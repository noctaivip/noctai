CONFIDENTIALITY GOVERNANCE LAYER

NDA Framework

Confidentiality within NOCTAI is governed through a structured, multi-stage non-disclosure framework. NDA instruments are not generic safeguards, but precision controls aligned with disclosure scope, timing, and jurisdiction.

1. Purpose of the Framework

The NDA Framework exists to protect structural integrity, capital positioning, and information asymmetry. It enables selective disclosure without compromising optionality or strategic reversibility.

2. Staged Confidentiality Model

Confidentiality obligations are introduced progressively, not universally.

  • Stage I — Contextual signals and high-level briefings (no NDA)
  • Stage II — Limited dossier disclosure (framework NDA)
  • Stage III — Transaction-specific materials (expanded NDA)
  • Stage IV — Bilateral agreements and execution (custom instruments)

3. NDA Issuance

NDA documents are issued only after preliminary alignment and internal qualification. NOCTAI does not distribute NDA templates publicly or on request.

Each NDA is tailored to the disclosure scope, jurisdiction, and capital role of the counterparty.

4. Use & Restrictions

Information disclosed under NDA may be used solely for internal evaluation purposes. Redistribution, derivative use, or disclosure to third parties is prohibited unless explicitly authorized.

5. Enforcement & Reversibility

Breach of confidentiality may result in immediate termination of access, withdrawal of materials, and legal remedies.

NOCTAI retains the right to suspend or reverse disclosure at any stage, irrespective of prior access.

6. Jurisdictional Structure

NDA instruments may be governed by different jurisdictions depending on the nature of the disclosure and the entities involved.

This page describes the confidentiality framework only. It does not constitute a legal agreement or disclosure instrument.
